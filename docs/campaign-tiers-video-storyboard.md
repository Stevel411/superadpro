## Section 3 — How a Single Tier Purchase Pays Out — 1:30 → 2:30

**Narration:**
> "Here's where it gets interesting. When someone buys a Campaign Tier — let's say they buy the hundred-dollar Pro tier — that hundred dollars gets split four ways."

**On screen:** Clean infographic. A central hundred-dollar bill or a $100 stack, then four arrows pointing outward, each labelled and animated to appear sequentially:

1. **40% to your direct sponsor** — $40
2. **50% spread up the team — eight levels deep** — $50
3. **5% platform fee** — $5
4. **5% bonus pool** — $5 (highlighted gold)

**Narration:**
> "Forty percent — forty dollars — goes straight to the person who referred the buyer. That's their direct sponsor commission, paid instantly.
>
> Fifty percent gets shared up the chain. SuperAdPro is an eight-level uni-level structure. That fifty percent is split equally — six and a quarter percent at each of the eight levels above the buyer. So eight different people in the upline each receive a piece of that purchase.
>
> Five percent is the platform fee — that's how SuperAdPro keeps the lights on, the servers running, and the platform improving.
>
> And the last five percent goes into a bonus pool. We'll talk about what that's for next."

**On screen:**
- After the four arrows are shown, zoom into the uni-level breakdown — show 8 stacked horizontal bars, each labelled "Level 1" through "Level 8" at "$6.25 each"
- Hold the visual for ~3 seconds so viewer can absorb

**Visual emphasis cue:** When mentioning "eight different people," briefly show 8 generic avatar circles arranged vertically, with $6.25 floating up to each.

## Section 4 — The Tier Qualification Rule — 2:30 → 3:00

**Narration:**
> "Now — there's one rule you need to understand. To earn commissions on a particular tier, you need to own an active campaign at that tier or higher. So if your downline buys the four-hundred-dollar Premium tier, but you only own the hundred-dollar Pro tier, you don't earn on that purchase."

**On screen:**
- Split visual: two member panels side by side
  - Left: "Your sponsor — owns Tier 5" → green checkmark, "$40 earned"
  - Right: "Your sponsor — owns Tier 3 only" → grey X, "Did not qualify"
- Subtle, no aggressive red — just neutral grey

**Narration:**
> "It works the same way for the eight uni-level positions. Each level is checked independently. So if you've got an active campaign at the level you need, you get paid. Simple as that. The system rewards you for keeping your own campaigns active — and that's a feature, not a quirk. It keeps the platform fair, and it keeps everyone genuinely engaged."

**Visual cue:** Show the SuperAdPro Campaign Tiers page with active-tier indicators (the new halo + ribbon design we shipped today). Brief but it ties video to product.

## Production Notes

### What to capture (screen recordings)

1. **Campaign Tiers page** at `/campaign-tiers` — wide hero shot showing all 8 tiers
2. **Single tier card hover state** — close-up showing one tier with name, price, views
3. **Active tier card** — featuring tonight's halo + ribbon + progress bar design (`Hybrid v1`)
4. **Tier modal** — clicking through to show the detail view with "Activate" button
5. **(Optional) Watch-to-Earn page** — to visually anchor what "delivered views" means

### Animation suggestions

- The four-way payout split (Section 3) is the most important graphic in the entire video. Spend the most production effort here. Recommended tools: After Effects, or even Keynote/Figma if budget is tight — clean shape animation matters more than polish.
- The 8-level uni-level visual benefits from a "cascading" reveal — viewer should physically count the eight steps.
- The completion bonus table (Section 5) should appear as a clean reveal, not all at once. Each row landing reinforces the scale.

### Music vibe

- Calm, modern, light cinematic — think Stripe product video, not crypto-bro EDM.
- No vocals. No big drops. Should sit underneath narration without competing.
- Royalty-free options: Epidemic Sound "Reflective Tech" category, Artlist "Corporate Modern" category.

### Voice direction

- Pace: ~150 words per minute. Confident, unhurried.
- Avoid hype words: "amazing", "incredible", "life-changing", "passive income", "easy money."
- Pause after each major number lands. Let the figure breathe.
- The tone is "I'm explaining how the engine works because I respect your intelligence" — not "click the link below."

### Things this video deliberately does NOT do

- ❌ Quote any potential earnings figures or income ranges
- ❌ Use the phrase "passive income"
- ❌ Use the phrase "recurring income" (technically wrong for Grid; reserve for Membership video)
- ❌ Promise outcomes ("you'll make $X in your first month")
- ❌ Mention what happens to commissions when a sponsor doesn't qualify (per locked spec rule — soft-pedal as "the system rewards keeping your campaigns active")
- ❌ Compare to other affiliate platforms or claim superiority

### Localisation note

Once approved in English, this script translates cleanly to all 20 supported locales. Numbers and tier names stay constant; only narration translates. Consider commissioning a Spanish version first as your second-largest member language base.
