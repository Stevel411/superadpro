The 200-subscriber newsletter that pays my rent

Alex Rivera · Jun 21, 2026 · 8 min read

I spent two years chasing followers before I realised the only number that actually mattered was the one I could email. Here's the whole story — and the single opt-in form that changed everything.

When I started, I did what everyone tells you to do. I posted daily. I chased the algorithm. I watched my follower count tick up and felt, for a while, like it was working. It wasn't. A good month of content would vanish the moment I stopped posting.

The shift that changed everything

The turning point was embarrassingly simple: I added an email signup to the bottom of every post. Not a popup, not a lead magnet funnel — just a quiet box that said "want the next one?"

The opt-in box, exactly as it appears at the end of each post.

Within a month I had more engaged readers on my list than I'd gathered in two years of chasing follows. And unlike followers, I owned the relationship.

"Followers are rented. An email list is owned. Build the thing you own."

That's the entire lesson. Everything else — the cadence, the subject lines, the offers — is detail. Start the list today, even if it's just you and your mum on it.
